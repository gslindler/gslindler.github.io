History of Atlanta

Atlanta, Georgia can be traced back to 1837. It marked the end of the Western & Atlantic railroad line. The first name of the city was Marthasville (named after the governor at the time's daughter). It was then changed to Atlanta because it was the feminine name for the Atlantic railroad. It started out as a major transportation hub and still is that way. Hartsfiled-Jackson International Airport is the busy airport in the world to this day. Atlanta is the capital of the South and has been since the beginning of its foundation.

The Georgia Aquarium

The Georgia Aquarium, loacted in Midotwn Atlanta next to Piedmont Park, is the largest aquarium in the world. It is the only aquarium outside of Asia that houses whalesharks! The biggest and most unique attraction is the Ocean Voyager Exhibit that conatains 6.3 million gallons of water where the whalesharks and hundreds of other sea creatures reside. The aquarium also has a new dolphin show exhibit and countless other sea animals, such as beluga whales and an iteractive starfish exhibit. The normal cost of a general admission tickets allows you to see all of this for $32.95!

Centennial Olympic Park

Centennial Olympic Park is a public park in Downtown Atlanta. It was originally created as a huge gathering spot for people to enjoy during the Summer Olympics in 1996. The park hosts many efvents for holidays, like the Fourth of July and has a Christmas-themed ice skating venue during the holiday season. The park remains a lasting memory of the Olympics in 1996 and also allows visitors and residents to enjoy a free and beautiful space in the middle of the city.

The World of Coca-Cola

The World of Coke (as many from Atlanta call it) is an interactive museum in Midtown that shows the history of the Coca-Cola company. There are many floors that each have their unique informative sessions. One of which being an IMAX movie for children to enjoy about the story of Coca-Cola. There is also a section where you can design your own label for your own Coke Bottle, and even a conveyor belt that shows you how the bottles are filled and made! The top floor is the most iconic featuret that allows you to taste different Coca-Cola products from all over the world!
